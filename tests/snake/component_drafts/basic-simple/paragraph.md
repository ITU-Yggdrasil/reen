# Paragraph

## Description
Paragraph is the long-form body copy component for multi-sentence content such as explanations, instructions, supporting detail, or editorial text. Use it when the content needs comfortable reading rhythm, and avoid using it for single-word labels or short UI metadata.

## Purpose
The purpose of Paragraph is to present longer blocks of readable content with a comfortable rhythm.

## Visual Structure
Paragraph is a block-level text element with consistent line length, spacing, and vertical rhythm. It should appear breathable and stable within a layout, with enough white space to support reading without feeling sparse.

## Subcomponents
None.

## States & Variants
- States: default, muted, inverted when used on dark surfaces.
- Variants: body, supporting, legal, intro.
- Alignment options: left aligned by default, centered only in deliberate marketing or callout contexts.

## Properties
- `content`: paragraph text.
- `variant`: tone and hierarchy within body copy.
- `alignment`: text alignment rule.
- `maxWidth`: readable measure for long-form content.
- `tone`: default or muted emphasis.
- `truncate`: whether overflow is allowed to be shortened in constrained layouts.

## Brand Reference
No formal brand specification is required for this draft. Paragraph should prioritize readability, comfortable spacing, and strong contrast over stylistic flourish. Presentation should feel calm, neutral, and easy to read in a wide range of interface contexts.
