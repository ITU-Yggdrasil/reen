# Text

## Description
Text is the general-purpose typographic primitive for short interface copy such as labels, captions, helper text, metadata, and inline messaging. Use it when content needs flexible presentation without the reading rhythm of a Paragraph or the strong hierarchy of a Heading.

## Purpose
The purpose of Text is to support short, flexible interface copy across a wide range of contexts.

## Visual Structure
Text may appear inline or as a small block, with styling determined by context and hierarchy. It should remain visually restrained and readable, acting as a dependable foundation for interface language rather than a decorative element.

## Subcomponents
None.

## States & Variants
- States: default, muted, emphasized, inverted, disabled when tied to an inactive control.
- Variants: body, caption, overline, helper, status.
- Weights: regular by default, medium or bold for emphasis when needed.

## Properties
- `content`: visible copy.
- `variant`: typographic role.
- `weight`: emphasis level.
- `tone`: default, muted, positive, warning, or critical where documented.
- `alignment`: inline or block alignment.
- `truncate`: whether long content should be shortened.
- `as`: semantic wrapper when the implementation later needs HTML or platform mapping.

## Brand Reference
No formal brand specification is required for this draft. Text should stay easy to scan, easy to understand, and visually restrained. Hierarchy should come from size, weight, spacing, and contrast rather than from decorative styling or invented brand tokens.
